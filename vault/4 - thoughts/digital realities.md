- **Real Reality (also called Real Virtual Environments):** Unlike traditional virtual reality which uses visual illusions, these are tangible, digitally generated facsimiles of real environments. They are made possible by **nanotechnology**, specifically "foglets" that can coalesce to form objects that not only look real but also possess tactile properties like hardness or wetness,. This technology eliminates the need for helmets or gloves because the environment itself is palpable.

#### Broken down this could be applied as a goal for object and furniture design - focussing on designing modular systems that enable transformative spaces for living and working instead of the good old Wandschrank or other isolated objects.

- **Neural Reality:** This reality is based on placing nano-scale sensors, known as **neural implants**, directly into the nervous system. These implants bypass our natural sensory organs (eyes, ears, skin, etc.) and transmit all five senses—sight, sound, touch, smell, and taste—directly to the brain. This provides the sensation of a "real" experience without the need for external displays or body suits.
